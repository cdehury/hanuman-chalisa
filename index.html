About Hanuman Chalisa

Shri Hanuman Chalisa is a hymn dedicated to Lord Hanuman, a prominent deity in Hinduism known for his strength, courage and devotion towards Lord Shri Rama.

Hanuman Chalisa was composed by the poet-saint Tulsidas in the 16th century in Awadhi language. Awadhi is a dialect of Hindi and is used in the northern India most importantly in Ayodhya also. Ayodhya is the birthplace of Lord Shri Rama. Shri Hanuman is known as the greatest devotee of Lord Rama for his unwavering devotion and selflessness. There are many other very well-known devotees of Lord Rama along with Shri Hanuman. Their names and roles are depicted in the Great Ramayana verbosely. We'll discuss about all of them one by one. Shri Hanuman followed Lord Rama's footsteps being a devoted companion. In Hinduism Shri Hanuman is the most powerfull God with divine powers and immortal. So the Hanuman Chalisa is being read as a hymn by every Hindu to feel the power of devoteness of Shri Hanuman. By reciting the Hanuman Chalisa devotees seek Shri Hanuman's blessings for various challenges endeavors in life.

Meaning of Hanuman Chalisa

The Hanuman Chalisa consists of 40 verses that's why it's known as Hanuman Chalisa. Chalis means 40 in Hindi and there's 40 verses so it's called as Hanuman Chalisa. So let's decode all verses one by one.

Shri Guru Charan Saroj Raj - Let's praises the lotus feet of the Guru who has guided the us to the right path.

Nij Manu Mukuru Sudhaari - It says that how Shri Hanuman cleansed his own mind with the name of Lord Shri Rama.

Baranau Raghubar Bimal Jasu - Poet describes Shri Hanuman as the best among all devotees of Lord Rama and says Shri Hanuman is the pure gem.

Jo Dayaku Phal Chaari - Here the poet says Shri Hanuman is the one who always ready to serve Lord Rama and provide the fruits of devotion.

Budhi Heen Tanu Jaanike - Poet says that even though Hanuman knows the body to be devoid of intelligence, he worships Lord Rama.

Sumirau Pavan Kumar - Poet encourages remembering Shri Hanuman, the son of the wind God, as a way to overcome all the difficulties.

Bal Buddhi Vidya Dehu Mohi - Requests Shri Hanuman to give strength, wisdom and knowledge.

Harahu Kalesa Vikara - Seeks Shri Hanuman's help in removing all afflictions, troubles and negative tendencies in life.

Jai Hanuman Gyan Gun Sagar - Praises Shri Hanuman as the ocean of virtues and wisdom.

Jai Kapis tihun lok ujagar - Acknowledges Shri Hanuman's glory as the illuminator of the three worlds(In Hindu Mythology).

Ram doot atulita bal dhama - Shri Hanuman, the messenger of Lord Rama, possesses unparalleled strength and is the abode of power.

Anjani putra Pavan sut nama - Son of Anjana and Pavana (wind god), known as Shri Hanuman.

Mahavir vikram Bajrangi - Shri Hanuman, the great hero with extraordinary valor.

Kumati nivar sumati ke sangi - Dispeller of evil thoughts, companion of the good-minded.

Kanchan varan viraj subesa - Adorned with a golden hue and elegant attire.

Kanan Kundal Kunchit Kesha - Wearing ear rings and having curly hair.

Hath Bajra Aur Dhvaja Viraje - Holding the thunderbolt and flag in hand.

Kandhe Moonj Janeyu Saaje - You are adorned with the sacred thread across your shoulder.

Shankar suvan kesari Nandan - Son of Shiva and Kesari (another name for Hanuman's father, Maruti).

Tej pratap maha jag vandan - The world praises your majestic aura and might.

Vidyavan guni ati chatur - Possessing knowledge, virtues and great intelligence.

Prabhu charittra sunibe ko rasiya - Delighted in listening to the stories of Lord Shri Rama.

FAQ's

Who is the author of Hanuman Chalisa?

Famous saint Tulsi Das have written Hanuman Chalisa. He was a great devotee of Lord Shri Rama. It was written back in the sixteenth century.

Who can read the Hanuman Chalisa?

Anyone, who is interested to know about Shri Hanuman and his abilities can read this chalisa. There is no such restriction on reading the Hanuman Chalisa in Hinduism.

How to read this Hanuman Chalisa?

If you are reading it for first time, you might find some difficulties while pronouncing the Hanuman Chalisa. Take a seat comfortably, keep your mind calm and try to read it. As it is written in the verses of this Hanuman Chalisa, It'll keep your mind calm and take you towards success as per the belief of great Hinduism.
